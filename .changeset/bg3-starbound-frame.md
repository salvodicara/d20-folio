feat(identity): the Starbound Frame — the hero-frame goldwork and the dialog seat divider redrawn from the owner's engraved plates

The reliquary corner unit (a small gem and one whisker arm, judged "not very nice" by the owner)
is superseded by the Starbound Frame: the anatomy of the owner's generated ornament plates —
compound hairline linework the owner liked, whose raster mounting had lost on physics — is
hand-authored as vectors drawn for the real seat scale. Each corner of the three earned hero
frames (framed realm masthead, cockpit identity band, dialogs) now seats a faceted four-point
star in a hairline diamond frame on the border vertex, launches a twin inner rail past a
dot-triplet, and bridges the rails with a concave gothic bracket; the twin rule continues through
the border-image edge slices as taper wedges, so it dissolves toward a long run's middle and
closes into a full double-ruled frame on dialogs. Dialog heads additionally seat the ceremonial
divider from the second plate — a small struck star in its diamond frame, flanked by leaf blades
and diamond nodes, occluding the fading seat rule behind it. Both ornaments keep the two-tone
strike (dark raised gold, light letterpressed bronze), stay decor-only, and never share air with
content ink; the ornament guard is rewritten to pin the new anatomy, including a drift guard on
the seat ornament's baked surface tone.
